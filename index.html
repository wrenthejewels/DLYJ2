Task mix and role fit

These direct `2.0` inputs distinguish what dominates your time from what defines the role's value.

Choose the task family that dominates your actual week, not the one that sounds most prestigious.

Use a second task family only if it represents a meaningful part of the role rather than occasional overflow work.

Pick the task family that most explains why the role exists, even if it is not the largest share of your week.

This should reflect current practical usage, not what the tools might be able to do in theory.

This is the direct decoupling question: if AI absorbs exposed tasks, does the remaining bundle still stand on its own?

Questionnaire

These questions personalize the selected occupation around exposure conditions, bundle integrity, adoption context, and fit.

Exposure Conditions 4 questions
1. Observed AI capability in this work

How well can current AI systems already handle the kinds of tasks most relevant to this occupation?

2. Example work and documentation

How much role-relevant example work exists for imitation, benchmarking, training, or structured review?

3. Benchmark and review clarity

How easy is it to tell whether work in this role is good, correct, or complete using clear criteria?

4. Task Digitization

What share of your work inputs and outputs already live in digital, text, spreadsheet, or system-readable form?

Bundle Integrity 6 questions
5. Task Decomposability

Can the work be broken into discrete pieces that can be delegated, reviewed, or benchmarked separately?

6. Task Standardization

How standardized are the procedures, templates, and workflow steps in this role?

7. Context Dependency

How much does good performance depend on local organizational context, unwritten norms, or situation-specific judgment?

8. Feedback and review speed

How quickly does the role usually get feedback that reveals whether output quality is acceptable?

9. Tacit Knowledge

How much of the expertise in this role is learned through experience rather than fully documented in explicit procedures?

10. Residual bundle fragility

If routine parts of the role were absorbed, how easily would the remaining work fragment or get redistributed instead of staying a distinct job?

Retained Work 2 questions
11. Human judgment and relationship load

How much does the role depend on trust, stakeholder sensitivity, negotiation, empathy, or human accountability?

12. Physical or on-site dependence

How much does the role depend on physical presence, site-specific work, or non-digitizable activity?

Adoption Context 4 questions
13. Organization AI adoption readiness

How prepared is your organization to integrate AI into actual workflows, not just experiment with demos?

14. Labor Cost Pressure

How strong is the pressure to reduce labor cost or increase output without adding headcount?

15. Labor Market Tightness

How difficult is it for employers like yours to hire and retain people with this skill mix?

16. Workflow integration readiness

How ready is the organization to plug new AI systems into the tools, data, and review loops this work depends on?

Personalization Fit 3 questions
17. Skill Transferability

How transferable are your core skills to adjacent work or to the retained version of this role if the bundle narrows?

18. Adaptability and learning speed

How quickly can you absorb new tools, workflows, and expectations when the role changes?

19. Relative performance in current role

How strong is your performance in this role relative to peers doing comparable work?

Understanding your output

Your `2.0` result is an empirical role briefing, not a certainty. It estimates which parts of your role are exposed, whether the change looks more like augmentation or automation, and whether the remaining bundle still looks like a viable job.

The main result is driven by O*NET task structure, Anthropic task-level evidence, and BLS labor-market context. Visit the Methodology for the pipeline and the Guide for how to interpret the briefing.
