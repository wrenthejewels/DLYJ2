# Fifth Role Transformation Calibration

Calibration batch: `fifth_tranche_2026_03`

This pass extends reviewed role-transformation calibration to the remaining eight launch occupations:
- Compliance Officers
- Logisticians
- Electronics Engineers, Except Computer
- Mechanical Engineers
- News Analysts, Reporters, and Journalists
- Public Relations Specialists
- Editors
- Statistical Assistants

The machine-readable overrides live in `data/metadata/pilot_role_transformation_calibration.csv`.

## Calibration intent

The reviewed adjustments are meant to:
- preserve human authority, liability, and escalation ownership in compliance work
- keep logistics in recomposition rather than collapsing it into generic administration
- preserve technical system ownership for electronics and mechanical engineering roles
- raise generation pressure for journalism while preserving reporting judgment and source work
- preserve relationship and reputation coordination for public-relations work
- keep editors under strong recomposition pressure without eliminating gatekeeping and standards
- increase compressibility and displacement pressure for statistical-assistant work relative to analyst roles

## Expected effects

- `Compliance Officers` should retain stronger accountability and lower displacement than generic policy administration.
- `Logisticians` should remain in recomposition while keeping supply, coordination, and tradeoff value.
- `Electronics Engineers, Except Computer` should show high tooling pressure but retained technical ownership.
- `Mechanical Engineers` should show similar retained ownership with slightly lower direct pressure than electronics engineering.
- `News Analysts, Reporters, and Journalists` should move toward heavier recomposition without collapsing source development and framing into automation.
- `Public Relations Specialists` should retain more reputation and stakeholder value than generic content-production roles.
- `Editors` should show strong recomposition pressure while preserving gatekeeping and quality-control value.
- `Statistical Assistants` should separate downward from analyst roles on compressibility and displacement.
