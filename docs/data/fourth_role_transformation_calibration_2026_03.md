# Fourth Role Transformation Calibration

Calibration batch: `fourth_tranche_2026_03`

This pass extends reviewed role-transformation calibration to the next seven launch occupations:
- General and Operations Managers
- Financial and Investment Analysts
- Operations Research Analysts
- Web Developers
- Graphic Designers
- Writers and Authors
- Advertising Sales Agents

The machine-readable overrides live in `data/metadata/pilot_role_transformation_calibration.csv`.

## Calibration intent

The reviewed adjustments are meant to:
- preserve operating ownership and resource decisions for operations managers
- preserve financial tradeoff judgment for financial analysts
- preserve decision interpretation for operations-research work despite high modeling exposure
- preserve web-platform ownership while raising tooling pressure for web developers
- raise generation pressure for graphic-design execution while keeping human brand direction
- raise generation pressure and recomposition for writing work
- preserve client relationship and revenue ownership for advertising-oriented sales work

## Expected effects

- `General and Operations Managers` should retain stronger human ownership than a pure workflow-admin reading suggests.
- `Financial and Investment Analysts` should remain in recomposition while keeping residual planning and resource-allocation value.
- `Operations Research Analysts` should show high exposure without collapsing model interpretation into automation.
- `Web Developers` should move toward higher direct pressure while retaining platform and release ownership.
- `Graphic Designers` should show stronger recomposition pressure than the function-heavy reviewed roles.
- `Writers and Authors` should move toward heavier recomposition than other reviewed content roles.
- `Advertising Sales Agents` should retain more bargaining and relationship value than generic support or clerical roles.
