Calibration batch: `third_tranche_2026_03`

This pass extends reviewed role-transformation calibration to the next five launch occupations:
- Human Resources Specialists
- Training and Development Specialists
- Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists
- Computer Systems Analysts
- Software Developers

The machine-readable overrides live in `data/metadata/pilot_role_transformation_calibration.csv`.

## Calibration intent

The reviewed adjustments are meant to:
- preserve manager coaching, employee trust, and policy judgment in HR work
- preserve program ownership for training roles even when learning-content production becomes more tool-assisted
- raise direct pressure for marketing analysis and marketing operations work
- preserve requirements translation and integration ownership for systems analysts
- preserve core engineering ownership for software developers despite high direct exposure

## Expected effects

- `Human Resources Specialists` should stay under recomposition pressure while retaining more trust and people-judgment value than generic workflow support.
- `Training and Development Specialists` should show stronger augmentation and recomposition rather than direct role collapse.
- `Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists` should move toward higher direct pressure and higher compressibility than the reviewed function-heavy occupations.
- `Computer Systems Analysts` should retain more function and accountability than a pure workflow-automation reading would imply.
- `Software Developers` should remain highly exposed but retain stronger system ownership and residual bargaining than the default first-pass output.
